Having worked at the popular wedding venue LEGACY ACRES in Conway, Arkansas for the past few years, Leah wasn't short on inspiration after viewing many different weddings. She'd spent the time brainstorming and dreaming up what she'd do for her own celebration one day. When Taylor proposed she already had so many different ideas of what she wanted - mainly something that hadn't been seen before yet still showcased who they are as a couple.

Captured by Arkansas wedding photographer ERIN WILSON, the venue held a special place in the bride's heart and it made perfect sense to get married there - its simple, elegant beauty sealed the deal. "The interior of the buildings are simply white with breathtaking chandeliers. Along with the Chapel and Ballroom the venue has a spacious cottage for couples and their wedding party to relax and get ready in. I never thought twice on where I would get married, I just always knew it would be at LEGACY ACRES."

When planning a special pair's dream wedding, Sojourner Auguste – founder and creative director of Erganic Design – worked her magic to create a gorgeous September soirée in New York City. "The couple wanted to find a raw-space venue that could be transformed into an immersive atmosphere... City views were also a must!" shares Sojourner.

Wanting to take full advantage of the city views from the rooftop of the Hudson Mercantile, both the ceremony and cocktail hour took place alfresco in order for loved ones to fully experience the iconic New York City skyline. The sunset ceremony overlooked a number of Manhattan’s landmarks, including the Hudson River, the famous red New Yorker sign, and of course, the Empire State Building.

Friends and family found their seats facing the city lights, and the spot for the vow exchange was marked with lush arrangements of pampas grass. "During the design process, it was clear the bride leaned toward modern design elements, but there was also an organic sensibility, which felt like a marriage of bohemian and whimsical," explains Sojourner, who not only planned the wedding but also designed the floral arrangements and décor. "The final design style we called, 'Modern Boho Whimsy.'"

guests enjoyed a rooftop cocktail hour complete with a Southern food station featuring artisan mac and cheese, fried chicken and waffles, and more

As loved ones entered the reception space, they were in awe of the stunning décor. The loft-style venue was adorned with décor in muted tones of grey and copper accented with taupe, putty, and blush, which was exactly what the bride envisioned. "She loved pampas grass and dusty miller, so we made sure to incorporate them into the floral arrangements," shares Sojourner of the desired look. "We created custom vases for the table centerpieces that were hand-sprayed with copper. These picked up the copper foil stamping of the dinner menus, as well as the copper charger plates and flatware."

Guest tables showcased either towering centerpiece arrangements of greenery, textured berries, Earl Grey and Quicksand roses, and pampas grass or low arrangements of similar florals and foliage. "We played up the ceiling height by creating hanging 'clouds' of pampas grass, greenery, florals, and lights," confirms Sojourner.
